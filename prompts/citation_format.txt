CITATION FORMAT (verbatim — do not deviate)

Every doctrinal reference uses TWO inline elements:

  1. Human-readable citation in square brackets, e.g.
       [JP 5-0, p. 42]
       [AAR Leader's Guide (2013), pp. 7–8]
  2. Immediately followed by a machine-checkable tag of the form
       {{cite:CHUNK_ID}}
     where CHUNK_ID is exactly one of the ids supplied to you in the RETRIEVED CHUNKS section below.

Full example:
    Pre-mission rehearsals reduce friction at first contact, consistent with doctrine [AAR Leader's Guide (2013), pp. 5–7]{{cite:aar_leaders_guide_2013:p5-7:c4}}.

Rules:
  - Do not cite chunks that were not retrieved.
  - Do not invent chunk_ids; copy them character-for-character from the RETRIEVED CHUNKS list.
  - Multiple citations per sentence are allowed, each with its own pair of brackets and tag.
  - One chunk may be cited multiple times across the section if relevant.
  - Do not include URLs in citations; the renderer adds them.
